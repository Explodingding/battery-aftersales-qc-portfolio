    Li-ion form factors — cylindrical vs prismatic vs pouch

    All three are lithium-ion — same electrochemistry, different mechanical packaging and factory assembly. For aftersales at Durapower (NMC & LFP · EV · ESS · UPS), the repair gate depends as much on construction as on chemistry.

    Internal structure — how they are built

    Every Li-ion cell shares cathode / separator / anode / electrolyte. What changes is how layers are assembled, how current is collected, and how the enclosure handles gas and swelling.

        Aftersales QC takeaway

        • Log IR per module and compute spread % (weak vs strong) at intake and after repair.
        • Verify BMS balancing reduces ΔV during test charge — not only “comm OK”.
        • Trend spread over cycles on repeat customers — widening spread predicts efficiency loss before catastrophic fail.
        • Compare production date / chemistry generation when assessing repaired packs — same form factor ≠ same fade behavior.
        • Match QC gate to form factor: cylindrical → string IR; prismatic → weld IR + compression; pouch → laminate + swell + frame torque.
        • LFP ESS repairs often involve prismatic modules — log compression shims at intake and release.
